Cheesesteak Recipe

Description

This cheesesteak recipe has been a family favorite for years and is our go-to meal for gatherings and special occasions. We start with premium filet mignon from a local butcher, carefully shaving and finely chopping the meat by hand for the perfect texture. The steak is cooked on a hot grill with a touch of olive oil, creating a juicy, flavorful bite while keeping the meat tender. We top it with sautéed green peppers, caramelized onions, and a combination of melted American and Cooper Sharp cheese for the perfect balance of creaminess and flavor. To finish it off, we lightly brush the roll with garlic butter, adding the perfect final touch to an unforgettable homemade cheesesteak.

Ingredients

Cheesesteak

  • 2lbs filet mignon
  • Fresh hoagie rolls
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 large green pepper
  • 1 large sweet onion
  • American cheese
  • Cooper sharp cheese
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Garlic Butter

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh parsley(optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder(optional, for extra flavor)

Optional Toppings

  • Sliced mushrooms
  • Banana peppers
  • Hot cherry peppers
  • Ketchup
  • Mayonnaise
  • Pickles

Steps

  1. Prepare the steak by placing the filet mignon in the freezer for 30-45 minutes. This makes it easier to shave into thin slices. Once chilled, thinly slice the meat and finely chop it.
  2. Prepare the vegetables by slicing the green bell pepper and sweet onion into thin strips.
  3. Make the garlic butter by combining the melted butter, minced garlic, parsley (if using), and garlic powder in a small bowl. Mix well and set aside.
  4. Heat the grill over medium-high heat and lightly coat the cooking surface with olive oil. Cook the onions and peppers until they are soft, lightly browned, and caramelized, about 6-8 minutes. Remove them from the grill and set aside.
  5. Cook the steak by spreading it evenly across the hot grill. Season with salt and black pepper, then continuously chop and stir the meat with a spatula until it is fully cooked and lightly browned.
  6. Combine the steak and vegetables by mixing the cooked onions and peppers back into the chopped steak.
  7. Add cheese by placing the American and Cooper Sharp cheese over the meat mixture. Allow the cheese to melt, then mix everything together until the meat is evenly coated.
  8. Toast the rolls by brushing the inside of each hoagie roll with the garlic butter. Place them on the grill for 1-2 minutes, or until lightly toasted and golden brown.
  9. Assemble cheesesteaksby filling each toasted roll with the hot steak, vegetable, and cheese mixture.
  10. Serve immediatelywhile the cheesesteaks are hot and the cheese is melted. Add optional toppings such as mushrooms, banana peppers, or hot cherry peppers if desired.
