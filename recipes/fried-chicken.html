Fried Chicken Recipe

Description

This is my grandmother's secret homestyle fried chicken recipe, passed down through our family for generations. Each piece is coated in a homemade seasoned breading made completely from scratch before being fried to a perfect golden crisp. The result is a juicy, tender chicken with a mouthwatering crunch in every bite. Packed with rich, savory flavor, this family recipe is one we believe stands above all other fried chicken recipes.

Ingredients

For Chicken

  • 3-4 lbs bone-in chicken pieces
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper

For homemade breading

  • 3 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch(for extra crispiness)
  • 2 teaspoons of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper(optional for a little heat)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon balck powder

For Frying

  • Vegtable Oil, peanut oil, or canola oil(personal choice olive oil)

Grandma's Secret Tip

  • Marinate the chicken in buttermilk, eggs, salt, and pepper for at least 4 hours, or overnight if possible. This helps tenderize the meat and keeps it incredibly juicy. For an extra crispy crust, let the breaded chicken rest for 10–15 minutes before frying so the coating adheres better to the chicken.

Steps

  1. Prepare the chicken by patting each piece dry with paper towels. In a large bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, eggs, salt, and black pepper. Add the chicken, making sure each piece is fully coated. Cover and refrigerate for at leats 4 hours, or preferably overnight.
  2. Prepare the breading by combining the flour, cornstarch, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper (if using), oregano, thyme, salt, black pepper, and baking powder in a large bowl. Mix well until all the seasonings are evenly distributed.
  3. Heat the oil in a large cast-iron skillet or deep fryer to 350°F (175°C). The oil should be deep enough to cover about half of each piece of chicken.
  4. Bread the chicken by removing each piece from the buttermilk mixture, allowing the excess to drip off. Coat the chicken thoroughly in the seasoned flour mixture, pressing the breading firmly onto the chicken for an even coating.
  5. Let the breaded chicken rest on a wire rack or baking sheet for 10-15 minutes. This helps the breading stick to the chicken during frying.
  6. Carefully place the chicken into the hot oil without overcrowding the pan. Fry in batches if necessary to maintain the oil temperature.
  7. Cook the chickenfor12-15 minutes per side turning occasionally, until the coating is golden brown and crispy. The internal temperature should reach 165°F(74°C).
  8. Remove the chicken from the oil and place it on a wire rack or a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.
  9. Allow the chicken to rest for about 5 minutes before serving. This helps the juices redistribute throughout the meat.
  10. Serve hot with your favorite side dishes such as mashed potatoes, coleslaw, macaroni and cheese, biscuits, or corn on the cob.

Grandma's Secret Tip

  • For the crispiest fried chicken, avoid flipping the chicken too often while frying. Let each side develop a deep golden-brown crust before turning it. Allowing the breaded chicken to rest before frying and letting it drain on a wire rack afterward helps keep the coating crispy instead of soggy.
